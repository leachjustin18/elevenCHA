Tunica adventita:
The outermost layer, the tunica adventita is primarily made up of loose connective tissue with a network of callagen and elastic fibers. These structures provide a protective mechanism to allow the vein to roll away from external trauma.
Tunica media:
The thick, middle layer of the vein made up of smooth and elastic muscle fibers which allow the vein to stretch or constrict in response to changes in blood volume, pressure or temperature.
Tunica intima:
The innermost layer, the tunica intima is a single layer of tightly configured endothelial cells which prevents fluid from escaping from the vein into the tissues.
